ECHOES OF GREAT ZIMBABWE

YEAR 2023
MEDIUM CORTEN STEEL
SCALE 14' X 8' X 6'

This piece serves as a structural interrogation of architectural memory. Inspired by the mortarless dry stone walls of Great Zimbabwe, I've reimagined these ancient geometries through the lens of industrial industrialism. The Corten steel, allowed to oxidize naturally, mimics the passage of time on granite, while the jagged, laser-cut apertures reference the loss and reclamation of history.

It is a monument to the endurance of form and the permeability of borders—both physical and temporal. The weight of the steel anchors the piece in the present, while its negative space invites the viewer to look through the metal into the void of what was left behind.
