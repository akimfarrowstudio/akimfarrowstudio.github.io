This painting begins with a photograph taken during a live performance by Vince Staples. The image was captured in the crowd and later translated into paint, carrying the energy of the concert into a slower process of reconstruction.

The composition centers on the act of performance. A raised microphone, the wash of stage lighting, and the presence of the DJ behind him establish the spatial rhythm of the stage. The bright lights above punctuate the darkness while color shifts across the figure’s skin and clothing, echoing the artificial lighting of the venue.

By repainting a moment originally recorded by a phone camera, the work studies how a fleeting experience becomes image and then object. The painting holds the memory of the concert while extending the moment through attention, color, and time spent rebuilding it by hand.
